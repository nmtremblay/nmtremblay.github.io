My Story

Like many of us, I didn't used to think about climate change.

But then, I went back to my childhood country, Brazil, to help take care of my cancer-stricken grandmother. My friends at school all lived in favelas, collections of self-constructed houses on the outskirts of the city. My mother once told me that if there was ever a hurricane, their houses would be the first to collapse.

I didn't think about that comment until I went back home and read The Uninhabitable Earth. It helped me realize that all my family and friends in Brazil were going to suffer through climate changes' worst effects. Crops in the tropics were at the highest risk of failing, their air was going to get the most polluted, and their houses would be the ones to collapse in a storm.

Since then, I started an effort to convert my school to 100% Renewable Energy (unfortunately interrupted by COVID-19). I've also begun researching AI and synthetic biology with The Knowledge Society. Right now, I'm most passionate about using living systems to mitigate some of climate change's worst side effects, like massive food shortages or unbreathable air.
